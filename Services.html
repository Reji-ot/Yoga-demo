The word Yoga is derived from the Sanskrit root ‘Yuj’, meaning to join or to unite. It’s widely accepted as the first mention of the word Yuj, was made in ‘Rig Veda’- One of the four Vedas written by the ancient Indian sages. While Yoga’s existence dates back to over 5000 years, it was the Indian Sage ‘Patanjali’ who systematised and documented Yoga practises before 2000 years. His Yoga sutras are identified as the foundation of classic yoga. The modern period of yoga saw great teachers like Ramana Maharishi, Raamakrishna Parmahansa, Yogananda Paramahansa, Vivekananda etc contributing to the development of Raja yoga. This was the period, when Vedanta, Bhakti Yoga, Hatha Yoga flourished. Now in the present times, everybody claims conviction about yoga, its maintenance and promoting as a way of healthy living. For many of us, Yoga is restricted is to Hatha yoga & asanas (poses), however Yoga extends itself way beyond the health & mental benefits it brings along. Yoga doesn’t attach itself to a particular religion, belief system or community and has always been acclaimed as a technology for inner well being. One who practises Yoga in its truest form will reap its benefits irrespective of any other factors. Our list of yoga services is as listed below:

Hatha Yoga

This lays the fundamentals of Yoga. Hatha is a combination of Ha- Sun & Tha-Moon, the principles of this Yoga is to bring balance between the sun & moon in you. It commences with preparations for your body (physical) to gradually move the complex yogic activities and practices.

Iyengar Yoga

Created and developed by renowned Acharya BKS Iyengar, these practices have been in practise for over 75 years. The principles focus on precision, timing and use of props such as bolsters, benches, straps and bricks to help learners experience the comprehensive benefits of poses (asanas) and practices. Iyengar Yoga, encourages you to practise to you own capacity and cautiously increase your capabilities.

Flow Yoga

A general term that refers to free style of yoga, wherein the practitioner moves dynamically from one asana to another, in a systematic way. This continuous flows creates a focus and lets the learn experience a meditative state. This yoga focuses on breathing (inhale & exhale) as a choreographed process while performing the asanas.

Wheel Yoga

A yoga wheel is a prop used by yoga practitioners to ease out the practise of various, otherwise complex poses. This style of yoga is gradually gaining popularity amongst learners, since its helps in relaxing and stretching the back, neck and shoulder muscles. Consistent practise of Wheel yoga brings about flexibility and helps in releasing muscular tension.

Chair Yoga

A more relaxed form of yoga, which focuses on accommodating learners from all spheres of life. This was introduced as a modified version of Hatha Yoga, for beginners and individuals with health complications.

Office Yoga

Office syndrome- A common in Office goers, caused by long hours of sitting or being in the same position. This causes contraction of muscles and causes repetitive muscle strains. To treat these, simple yoga forms were designed that could be performed at the comfort of your office chair.

Pregnancy Yoga

Modified yoga postures to create balance between body and mind during one of the important phases of women’s life. These postures, when performed under strict guidance and support from our gurus will help you during your labour.

Yoga Cleansing

Yogic cleansing (Shat kriyas) processes are integral part of yoga that helps to eliminate accumulated toxins from our bodies. Correct practise helps in cleansing toxins like mucus, acidity, sweat, urine and stool and improve the overall functioning of our internal organs. These kriyas also prepares your body to practise yoga asanas in the right form.

Pranayama


Pranayama is a combination of two Sanskrit words- ‘Prana’- meaning life force and ‘Ayama- meaning to restrain or to draw out. The combined form- Pranayama- rough translates into breath control. It is a practice in yoga that regulates your breathing through specific techniques and practices. It helps to relax the body and mind through a combination of inhaling, exhaling and control of breath. One of the earliest recorded references about Pranayama can be found in Brihadaranayka Upanishad linking it to breathing and regulating vital energies.
Breathing is unique as compared to other bodily functions. Though it’s a natural process, it can also be controlled voluntarily. Acts such as singing, playing oral instruments and singing are some of the examples of controlled breathing process. In Yogic principles, pranayama is combined with physical postures (asanas) and meditation. Together, these results in greater benefits for the practitioner. Panayama can be performed while doing asanas, meditating or by itself. Studies have shown that persistent practise of Pranayama brings wholesome benefits to mind, body and soul.

Myriad advantages of practising Pranayama.

  • Reduces Stress: Pranayama helps in calming the nervous system and thus helps in decreasing stress levels. This is mainly result of better oxygen intake during the practise of pranayama.

  • Improves sleep quality: A study shows that practising Pranayama improved the sleep quality in people with obstructive sleep apnea. It further showed that Pranayama also helped in reducing snoring and other breath related issues.

  • Increases mindfulness: Breathing, for most of us is an automatic process and doesn’t need to be given a thought. However, studies have shown that people who practise pranayama have greater levels of mindfulness. This was associated with the calming effects of pranayama.

  • Reduces hypertension: Stress poses as a major risk and contributes in increasing blood pressure. Regular practice of Pranayama calms the mind and helps in reducing the blood pressure by a great extent.

  • Improves lung function: Continuous practise of Pranayama helps in improving multiple parameters of lung function. It strengthens the lung against various ailments like asthama, allergice pneumonia, bronchitis and more.

Meditation


Some archaeological studies state that meditation was practised as early as 5000BC. It has deep rooted connections with the ancient Egypt and China. It is also associated with Judaism, Hinduism, Jainism & Buddhism. Meditation is known as the practise of focussed concentration. Much like Pranayama, meditation also helps in relieving stress, calms nerves, and heals number of ailments.
